FAQ

Common questions.

Do I need a medical background to volunteer?

Not at all. Bright Stays volunteers are compassionate people who love working with children. We provide all the training you need — including hospital etiquette, child development basics, and activity facilitation.

How often do volunteers visit?

We ask for a commitment of at least one visit per week. Sessions are typically 1–2 hours, scheduled around hospital access windows and the volunteer's availability.

Is Bright Stays free for families?

Yes, completely. Our services are free to all patients and families. We're funded through donations, grants, and institutional partnerships — never through patient fees.

Which hospitals are you in?

We're currently piloting at Boston Children's Hospital, with NYC expansion planned for Summer 2025 across NYU Langone, Columbia, and NewYork-Presbyterian. More cities to follow.

Can I volunteer remotely or virtually?

Our core program is in-person — bedside visits are central to building real connections. That said, we're exploring supplemental virtual programming and always welcome ideas. Reach out!

How do I know if my hospital qualifies?

If your hospital has a pediatric inpatient ward and a child life program (or is open to starting one), it likely qualifies. Send us a message and we'll explore it together.
